Why Exterior Paint Fails

Prep Is What Makes It Last

Most exterior paint jobs that fail after 2–3 years weren't painted badly — they were prepped badly. Paint applied over peeling old paint, cracked caulk, bare wood, or dirty siding simply can't adhere properly and will fail the same way.

We take exterior prep seriously. Every surface gets scraped to remove loose and peeling paint, sanded to a stable edge, and cleaned before anything else happens. Gaps and cracks around trim, windows, and doors get caulked. Bare wood gets primed. Then we paint.

We use high-quality exterior paints rated for Western New York's freeze-thaw cycles — the kind of formulation that flexes with your home instead of cracking when temperatures drop.

Our Process

Built to Last Through WNY Winters

  1. 01

    Wash & Clean

    We start with a thorough pressure wash or scrub to remove dirt, mildew, and chalk from the existing surface. Paint won't bond to a dirty surface.

  2. 02

    Scrape & Sand

    All loose, peeling, and flaking paint is removed. Edges are feathered smooth so the new paint goes on evenly without visible edges or ridges.

  3. 03

    Caulk & Repair

    Gaps around trim, windows, doors, and siding seams are sealed with flexible exterior caulk. Any rotted or damaged wood is flagged and repaired before painting over it.

  4. 04

    Prime Bare Surfaces

    Any raw wood or areas taken back to bare material get primed before the finish coat. This step is critical for adhesion and even color coverage.

  5. 05

    Apply Finish Coat(s)

    We apply finish paint in even, consistent passes. Most work gets two coats. We don't leave until the coverage is even, the color is solid, and the edges are clean.
